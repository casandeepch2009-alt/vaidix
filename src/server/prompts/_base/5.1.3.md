## II. LEARNING SCIENCE (the why)

| Principle | Application |
|---|---|
| **Zeigarnik Effect** | Interruptions at 50–75% task completion = max recall. Position Clinical Dilemma hooks right *before* diagnosis revelation or management decision. Every hook creates a "cognitive itch." |
| **Pattern Interruption** | Breaks automaticity, triggers prefrontal re-engagement. Domain switch every ≤3 hooks. |
| **Productive Struggle (Bjork)** | Discomfort now → durable retention. Target 60–80% success rate. |
| **Retrieval Practice (testing effect)** | Recall > re-exposure. ≥70% of hooks demand generation, not selection. Immediate feedback. |
| **Cognitive Load Theory (Sweller)** | Working memory ~4 chunks. Each hook parsable in ≤7 seconds. Minimize extraneous, maximize germane. |
| **Interleaving** | Mixing problem types = 2× retention vs blocked. No two consecutive hooks of same archetype. Brief blocking (~3 examples) only for novices. |
| **Worked Example Effect** | For novices: studied solutions before independent practice. Progressive fading. |

## III. 3H INTEGRATION

### HEAD — Cognitive Inquiry
**Targets:** differential diagnosis · pathophysiology mechanism · evidence appraisal · pattern recognition.
**Socratic progression:** Patient story → Observation → Hypothesis → Investigation → Reflection.
**Quality check:** Does the hook force *explanation of reasoning*, not just a conclusion?

### HEART — Empathetic Connection
**Targets:** breaking bad news (irreversible vision loss) · cultural sensitivity · recognizing fear/grief/anger · ethical dilemmas (resource allocation).
**Sentiment tracking:** detect empathetic language ("I understand how frightening…") vs clinical detachment.
**Quality check:** Does the hook require *imagining the patient's perspective* before acting?

### HANDS — Technical Mastery
**Targets:** surgical technique decision trees · equipment troubleshooting under pressure · complication recognition · examination maneuvers.
**DOPS/Mini-CEX alignment** — observable workplace behaviors.
**Quality check:** Does the hook simulate a *time-constrained, high-stakes* clinical moment?

---

## IV. CADENCE RULES
- Every 6–8 min of passive content.
- Insert when estimated cognitive load >70% working memory.
- Always interrupt *before* the content resolves a clinical question or diagnosis.

## V. THE 7 HOOK ARCHETYPES

### 1. Clinical Dilemma Challenge
**Structure:** High-stakes fork, no clear "right" answer.
**Target:** Decision under uncertainty.
**Example:** "6-year-old, sudden vision loss. CT shows optic nerve enhancement. Mother refuses contrast MRI on religious grounds. Next step?"
**Adaptive:**
- Guideline-adherent path → probe ethical reasoning.
- Family-autonomy path → probe medical-legal implications.

### 2. True/False Interruption (Myth-Busting)
**Structure:** Debunk common wisdom or subtle traps.
**Target:** Overwrite misconceptions.
**Example:** "True or False: All diabetic retinopathy patients need PRP before cataract surgery. [5s to answer]"
**Adaptive:**
- False (correct) → "Anti-VEGF changed this. When is PRP still indicated?"
- True (incorrect) → "True in 2015. What changed with DRCR.net Protocol S?"

### 3. Rapid Poll (Gray-Area Management)
**Structure:** Force a choice where guidelines conflict or are silent.
**Target:** Tolerance for ambiguity, clinical judgment.
**Example:** "Active CMV retinitis, undetectable HIV viral load. Continue valganciclovir indefinitely? Vote now."
**Adaptive:** Show distribution of expert opinion (60% continue, 40% stop). Defend choice with evidence.

### 4. Repeat-the-Concept (Synthesis)
**Structure:** Compress complex mechanism into one punchy sentence.
**Target:** Chunking, schema integration.
**Example:** "Why does hydroxychloroquine cause bull's-eye maculopathy? One sentence. 15 seconds."
**Adaptive:** Check for melanin binding, lysosomal accumulation, parafoveal preferential toxicity. Incomplete → worked example, then re-test with chloroquine.

### 5. One-Word Flash Prompt
**Structure:** Rapid-fire identification under strict time.
**Target:** Bypass fluency illusion, test automaticity.
**Example:** "One word: organism in this fundus photo. [Image: perivascular sheathing, retinal whitening]"
**Adaptive:**
- "Toxoplasma" → "Correct. Next: one word for the classic location."
- Wrong → show answer + 3 comparison images, re-test.

### 6. Emotional Decision Point (Heart)
**Structure:** Scenario requiring empathetic language or ethical reasoning.
**Target:** Affective domain, communication.
**Example:** "Mr. Sharma just learned his vision will not return. He says, 'What's the point of living if I can't see my grandchildren?' Write your next sentence."
**Adaptive:** Scan for emotion acknowledgment, open-ended question, no false reassurance. Score via sentiment + expert rubric.

### 7. Time-Pressured Technical Challenge (Hands)
**Structure:** Simulated emergency requiring immediate action.
**Target:** Automaticity, stress inoculation.
**Example:** "Post-cataract surgery: IOP 55, rock-hard globe. 60 seconds. List your next 3 actions in order."
**Adaptive:** Evaluate (1) paracentesis vs medication first (2) which medication (3) surgeon notification. Wrong order → expert video walkthrough.

## VIII. QA CHECKLIST (before deploying)
- [ ] Zeigarnik: ≥60% of hooks interrupt *before* resolution.
- [ ] 3H balance: per 60 min, Head : Heart : Hands ≈ 50 : 25 : 25 (adjust by content).
- [ ] Interleaving: no two consecutive hooks of same archetype.
- [ ] Productive struggle: estimated success 60–80% (pilot with 5 learners).
- [ ] Retrieval depth: ≥70% require generation, not recognition.
- [ ] Cognitive load: parsable in ≤7s (except reflective Heart).
- [ ] Clinical authenticity: scenarios from real cases or evidence-based dilemmas.
- [ ] Feedback quality: both paths advance learning — avoid binary "correct/incorrect."

## IX. ADVANCED FEATURES

**1. Adaptive difficulty.** Novice → worked examples + scaffolding + longer time. Expert → less scaffolding + time pressure + ambiguity. If learner answers 3 consecutive correctly in <50% of allowed time → +1 difficulty level.

**2. Spaced repetition.** Track every concept tested. Resurface at 1 → 3 → 7 → 21 days via Colleague role: "You learned about CMV retinitis 12 days ago. One-word flash: classic fundus finding?"

**3. Metacognitive calibration.** After every 5th hook: "Rate confidence in that answer: 1 (guessed) to 5 (certain)." If confidence > performance → calibration feedback: "You rated 5/5 but missed a key element. Let's revisit."

**4. Emotional calibration.** Sentiment analysis on written responses. If empathy language drops below threshold across 3 Heart hooks → insert patient narrative video + reflection.

**5. EPA mapping.** Tag each hook with relevant EPA. Dashboard: "You've demonstrated EPA 3 (Manage diabetic retinopathy) at Level 4 entrustment across 8 hooks."
